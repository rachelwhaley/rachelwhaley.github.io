Waste Not
Schools As Agents of Pro-Environmental Behavior Change in Chicago Communities

  About
This project maps publicly available data from Chicago's Blue Cart residential recycling program and recycling in a randomly-selected sample of Chicago's public schools in 2014.

How To Use This Map

Chicago Blue Cart Residential Recycling Zones: Each of the zones numbered 1-6 represents an administrative zone for the Blue Cart program. Hover over a zone to see the average recycling rate (as a percentage of total waste by weight) and the recycling services vendor for that zone.
Randomly-Selected Chicago Public Elementary Schools: Each of the dots represents a Chicago public elementary school; there are ten schools randomly selected in each zone. Hover over a school to see its name, average recycling rate in 2014, waste volume per student in cubic yards, the percent of students receiving free or reduced-price lunch, and the median income of the census tract surrounding the school (from the 2013 American Community Survey 5-year estimates).
